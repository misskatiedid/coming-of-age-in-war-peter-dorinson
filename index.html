Coming of Age in War

Exploring Peter Dorinson's Youth in the 1960s

By Katherine Dorinson

About Peter

Peter is a UC Berkeley Alumni, retired lawyer and real estate developer, father of two and a Vietnam War Veteran. His unique experience with the draft in the mid-1960s would forever alter his university experience and shape his adult life.

In this interview, Peter discusses his life leading up to his time in Vietnam. He also describes his experience in the Navy and how he remained positive when he held little control over his future.

Photo Gallery

Peter (upper left) with his Fraternity Brother class at UC Berkeley in 1963. He recalls about 40% of the men in this picture were drafted and served in Vietnam.

Semester abroad turned 1A classification: While he was a registered university student, Peter received a "Notice to Appear For Physical Exam" in the mail. After negotiating with the draft board, he agreed to serve three years in the Navy and trained at Treasure Island on a weekly basis throughout his senior year. What began as a clerical error turned into a journey that would forever interrupt his university experience and young adult life.

Peter describes his final year at Berkeley as overwhelmingly daunting. Optimism was paramount as he was uncertain of what his future would hold. Peter did not walk at his graduation.

Peter served as an officer for two years on the USS St. Paul and one year in Da Nang, RVN.

After being discharged, Peter was not eager to return to the United States. He took the LSAT in Da Nang and then spent three months skiing in Australia. He started law school at UC Hastings in September of 1970.

Peter is well known by family and friends as a history buff.

Words of wisdom: Peter says that in life you have to make the best of situations. "I knew I was going to serve 3 years one way or another, so I just made the best of it."

Peter smiles with a photo collage of him on the USS St. Paul. These distant memories shaped a large part of his adult life.
